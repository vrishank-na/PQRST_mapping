## My prediction (before seeing the results)

I actually think Focal Loss has a **better chance** of helping now than it did previously.

Here's why.

In the original model:

* QRS was already extremely easy.
* Focal Loss kept emphasizing the difficult P/T regions **and** still had to balance the dominant background and QRS classes.

Now:

* QRS has been removed.
* The remaining difficult classes are exactly the ones we care about.
* Hard examples are mostly ambiguous P/T boundaries.

That is much closer to the scenario Focal Loss was designed for.

I'm **not** predicting a dramatic jump—don't expect P F1 to suddenly become 0.90 on LUDB—but a **1–3 percentage point** improvement in macro F1 would not surprise me.

## One more suggestion

Since each run only costs about an hour, save **every checkpoint and every evaluation**. Even if Focal Loss ends up slightly worse, that negative result is useful. It strengthens your conclusion that **the primary gain came from the R-centred physiological representation**, not from changing the optimization objective.

Ironically, the "failed" optimization experiments may end up making your paper stronger, because they demonstrate that the improvement wasn't due to hyperparameter luck—it came from a principled reformulation of the task.
