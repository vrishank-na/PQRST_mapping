## The QTDB annotation issue is also worth discussing.

This is something I started suspecting when you mentioned the missing T annotations and the strange behavior of records like `sel41`.

There are two different kinds of "ground truth":

1. **Annotation quality** (are the labels themselves accurate?)
2. **Dataset difficulty** (does the dataset contain pathological morphologies?)

If the annotations are inconsistent, then no model can achieve perfect agreement with them. In fact, a model that disagrees with a noisy label can sometimes be *more physiologically correct*.

So if you're seeing things like:

* inconsistent T boundaries,
* missing T onset/offset annotations,
* visually obvious boundaries that don't match the labels,

then the evaluation ceiling on QTDB may simply be lower than we expect.
